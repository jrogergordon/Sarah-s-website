A freeform journal

The idea of a website for your birthday seemed cute. So I made one. I wasn't sure exactly what I wanted to put on it. I knew I wanted to show you my recording from when I was sick, but after that I didn't have concrete ideas. I like keeping records so I thought about incorporating a summary, from my POV, of some of the best moments from our relationship. From there I brainstormed some ideas and settled on something like an online journal with a few entries in different styles. I've always had immense respect for the privacy your journal commands and because of that when you share pieces with me it's very rewarding. I hope this gift imparts something like that. Below you'll find a summary of the various journal like things you'll find on the site:

First: A journal entry! This is very personal and I don't know how you'll feel about it or if you'll even want to read. Everything else on this site I made with your enjoyment in mind but the point of this is specifically that it isn't made for you, it's something I made for me that I'm sharing with you.

So, I looked back through our texts and found some fun ones.

1.5. A fun fact

     I might have mentioned this to you. Sometime early in life I read that the average time it took for a couple to say "I love you" was six months. With my previous girlfriends I've tried to wait until after 6 months to say it, I'm not sure why. Previously it didn't matter that much to say it or not say it, and waiting for an arbitrary amount of time wasn't even something I thought much about, it was just a rule I decided to follow. With you there wasn't time for that thought to occur in my head. Days after we first had sex my heart was drawn to yours in a way I didn't know it could be pulled. "I love you" danced in my head and waited on my tongue in a way I hungered to express. You were the first person to give me a reason to say "I love you." Now I know what it feels like to want to say that first. Did I say "first?"
